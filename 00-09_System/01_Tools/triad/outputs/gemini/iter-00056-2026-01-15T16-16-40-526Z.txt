- **Observation**: A significant portion of the file structure consists of `mpdm-` (Multi-Party Direct Message) folders (e.g., `mpdm-clcochr--ron--...`). Combined with the 58% DM rate, this indicates that **key operational decisions and coordination are happening in ad-hoc, private groups** rather than transparent public channels.
- **Key Players**: The "Inner Circle" visible in MPDM names includes **Ron**, **Chris Cochran (clcochr)**, **Emily Humphrey**, and **Jen**.
- **Impact**: This "Shadow Factory" creates a knowledge silo where strategic context is trapped in private history, inaccessible to new hires or the "Triad" system itself.

### The "Ghost Tools" (Triad & Maestro)
- **Observation**: Despite being a major focus of Ron's *System* and *Tooling* (as seen in search indexes), a full-text search of Slack reveals **zero mentions of "Triad"** and only incidental/unrelated mentions of "Maestro" (referring to a podcast guest).

### Operational Friction as Communication Noise
- **Observation**: In `production-sync` (e.g., `2025-06-18.json`), urgent threads ("ASAP") are triggered by basic process failures, such as missing video assets (`.braw` files).
- **Insight**: Slack is being used as a **high-latency patch for Digital Asset Management (DAM)**. The team uses chat to locate files that should be in a deterministic system, creating unnecessary "false urgency" and stress.

## 2. Strategic Insights

- **The "Bus Factor" Risk**: The heavy reliance on MPDMs means that if Ron or Chris were unavailable, the "connective tissue" of the projects (context, decisions, unwritten rules) would be largely invisible to the rest of the team.
- **Development Disconnect**: The `00-development` channel is sporadic. If "Triad" is an engineering effort, it is happening outside the visible development lifecycle of the company, potentially limiting its adoption or integration with actual team pain points.
